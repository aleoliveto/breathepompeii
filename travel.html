- Travel to Pompeii

By Train

If you are starting from Rome, you can take a train from Rome's main train station, Roma Termini, to Pompeii. The journey takes about an hour and a half, and trains run frequently throughout the day. Once you arrive at Pompeii, you can take a short walk to the archaeological site.

If you are starting from Naples, you can take a train from Naples' main train station, Napoli Centrale, to Pompeii. The journey takes about 20 minutes, and trains run frequently throughout the day. Once you arrive at Pompeii, you can take a short walk to the archaeological site.

By Guided Tour

If you prefer a guided tour, you can book a tour that includes transportation from either Rome or Naples to Pompeii. These tours often also include a guided visit of the site and can be a great way to learn more about the history and significance of Pompeii.

By Car

If you are driving, you can take the A3 motorway and exit at Pompeii, then follow the signs to the site. There is a big parking lot close to the entrance of the site.

You will need to purchase a ticket to enter the archaeological site, and it is recommended to book in advance, as the site can get quite crowded. It is also worth noting that Pompeii is a large and spread-out site, so it can take several hours to explore fully. Comfortable shoes and sunscreen are recommended.
